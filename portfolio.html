Bee 'n Bee Hotel in Blender

Date: 22 - 3 - 2023

For this module's project, my group and I tackled the issue of the declining bee population. For one of the deliverables, we had to make a lo-fi prototype of our product. This included a visualisation of a bee hotel, which I made in blender. The wooden textures were rendered by sticking pictures of pieces of wood on meshes, and I am rather satisfied with the end result!

Project Ducks 'n Explosions

Date: 7 - 1 - 2023

For the final project of Module 5, we had to make a simple video-game. Me and my friend worked together on our game called Ducks 'n Explosions. The game's objective is to rescue a duck who got stuck inside a school and get him outside safely. However, the only means of interacting with the game is by throwing bombs and creating explosions, from which the duck will run away. We divided the task in two parts: the design and the technical part. I worked on the design part, which includes making all objects and characters in blender, importing and putting them together in Unity and creating the sound-effects and music. you can play the game here!

Blender Pocketwatch

Date: 1 - 4 - 2022

This module we had the course Interactive Visualisation. We did this by following some lectures and by doing the weekly assignments. These showed you the basic knowledge that you needed to work with these programs. For the final assignment, you had to make an object in Blender, where you would be graded on how well you met the the needed requirements. I decided to make a pocketwatch as my final project, since I though this would be interesting and challenging. I had an advantage that I already had some Blender experience from the previous module's ProfDev challenge. I looked up lots and lots of pictures of pocketwatches and watched video's on how they technically worked, and manage to make a design that in a way contains some certain aspects of a real working pocketwatch. For the materials I looked up online how to make those, and since Blender has a big community, there is always a tutorial that explains exactly what you're looking for. The TA's were (if I may say so) quite astonished by my final product: especially the detail and the modelling were remarkable according to them. I was rather happy with a 9/10 for my final result!

Blender Donut

Date: 22 - 1 - 2022

For this module's Professional Development challenge, we had to dive into a topic that we thought to be interesting. I decided to take a look into the world that is Blender. Blender is a computer graphics software that people can use for the visualisation of all sorts of things: it can be used to make realisticly looking artificial images, animation, games, virtual reality etc. I followed a YouTube tutorial by Blender Guru, consisting of 16 video's in which he shows the different tools that Blender provides. During this tutorial he'll show how to use those tools to create a realisticly looking Donut. Since Blender is a rather extensive program, the possibilities are limitless and you can experience with it all you want. Since it is almost impossible to learn every bit of Blender, this would also be the way to become good at it: by getting familiar with the program.

The ring

Date: 21-10-2021

I am a big fan of the Lord of the Rings and The Hobbit, and some time ago I thought it would be cool to have a ring of my own. So I made one of a nut I found in the shed. I started with filing it to make it more round, and gave it a shiny surface using very smooth sandpaper. The nut was made out of stainless steel and very tough; this project took me around 20 hours to finish.
