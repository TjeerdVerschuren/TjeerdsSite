NucleArt / 22 - 6 - 2022
For this module's project, we were given the task to create an interactive art installation. This would be meant to be put at the punk festival in enschede called Gogbot. We decided to handle the topic of the dangers of nuclear war. Our plan is to make an Arcade, which has three buttons and a screen on it. When playing the game, you are in command of an army during a war. In the beginning, you will get a little overview on the screen on what has been going on. The war has only just begun at this point. As you play the game, it is up to you to make decisions about the war. These will be sort of dilemma's: you will have to choose between two options, both with their upsides but also with their terrible negative effects. The choices you have to make are getting more difficult and darker as you play. We eventually want to have the player think about the effects of this war, think about what they had just done, feel disturbed and how war is in fact not a game, allthough it is displayed as an arcade. I mostly worked on the ideation phase and the building of the arcade and the story.

Blender Pocketwatch / 1 - 4 - 2022
This module we had the course Interactive Visualisation, where we learned how to work with the softwares Blender and Unity.
We did this by following some lectures and by doing the weekly assignments. These showed you the basic knowledge that you needed to work with these programs. For the final assignment, you had to make an object in Blender, where you would be graded on how well you met the the needed requirements. I decided to make a pocketwatch as my final project, since I though this would be interesting and challenging. I had an advantage that I already had some Blender experience from the previous module's ProfDev challenge. I looked up lots and lots of pictures of pocketwatches and watched video's on how they technically worked, and manage to make a design that in a way contains some certain aspects of a real working pocketwatch. For the materials I looked up online how to make those, and since Blender has a big community, there is always a tutorial that explains exactly what you're looking for. The TA's were (if I may say so) quite astonished by my final product: especially the detail and the modelling were remarkable according to them. I was rather happy with a 9/10 for my final result!

Blender Donut / 22 - 1 - 2022
For this module's Professional Development challenge, we had to dive into a topic that we thought to be interesting. I decided to take a look into the world that is Blender.
Blender is a computer graphics software that people can use for the visualisation of all sorts of things: it can be used to make realisticly looking artificial images, animation, games, virtual reality etc. I followed a YouTube tutorial by Blender Guru, consisting of 16 video's in which he shows the different tools that Blender provides. During this tutorial he'll show how to use those tools to create a realisticly looking Donut. Since Blender is a rather extensive program, the possibilities are limitless and you can experience with it all you want. Since it is almost impossible to learn every bit of Blender, this would also be the way to become good at it: by getting familiar with the program.
